## 🧰 Need help structuring your thinking?

If you're feeling overwhelmed or unsure what to do, use this:

👉 [⚡ Incident Response Tool](https://malikagarg.xyz/prompt-library/incident-response.html)

It helps you slow down and organise your thoughts before taking action.

---

## 1. How serious is this?

- Risk of escalation?
- Crowd / retaliation / panic?
- Vulnerable victim?
- Public or sensitive location?
- Weapons involved?

👉 Decide: **How much control is needed right now**

---

## 2. Get minimum facts

- What happened?
- Where?
- Still ongoing?
- Who is on the ground?

👉 Enough to act. Not everything.

---

## 3. Stabilise immediately

- Send force
- Ensure presence
- Coordinate (medical / fire / magistracy)

👉 Nothing should worsen from here

---

## 4. Prevent early damage

- Crowd / panic / retaliation
- Evidence loss
- Rumours

👉 These matter more than investigation right now

---

## 5. Ensure clear command

- One officer in charge
- Clear responsibility
- Reinforce if needed

---

## 6. Communicate clearly

- Short instructions
- No ambiguity

👉 Clarity = control

---

## 7. Control messaging

- Facts only
- Avoid speculation
- Say less early

👉 Show control, not confusion

---

## Exit

Move on only when:
- situation stabilised  
- threat neutralised  

---

**In one line:**  
Control first. Everything else later.
