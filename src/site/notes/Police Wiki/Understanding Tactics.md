## What is a Tactic?

Every real skill in policing — reading a crowd, running an investigation, keeping your station in shape — reduces to a small number of things you're actually trying to do. A Tactic is one of those things: not a department, not a legal section, not a type of case, but a genuine operational question every officer answers dozens of times a week, often without naming it.

👉 The test for a Tactic: **what is this piece of knowledge actually helping the officer do?**

"Always know which constables belong to which villages" isn't about any one case — it's [[Police Wiki/Tactics/Gathering Information\|Gathering Information]]. "Never let two hostile groups leave through the same gate" is [[Police Wiki/Tactics/Securing & Controlling\|Securing & Controlling]]. "Brief the media before rumours fill the vacuum" is [[Police Wiki/Tactics/Influencing & Shaping Behaviour\|Influencing & Shaping Behaviour]]. Once you can name the tactic, you can find every technique, checklist, and real example filed under it — instead of hunting through old case files or asking around.

---

## Why organise this way?

Most police knowledge gets filed by *what happened* — riot, murder, VIP visit, flood. But the same underlying skills get used across all of them. A murder investigation and a riot both require gathering information, building situational awareness, planning, deploying resources, commanding execution, influencing people, securing a scene, keeping a record, supervising the work, leading the team, and learning afterwards. Organise by tactic instead of by incident type, and what you learn from one situation actually transfers to the next one — even one that looks nothing like it on paper.
