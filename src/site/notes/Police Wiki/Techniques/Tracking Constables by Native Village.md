**What this is:** Keep an informal roster of the officers and constables serving under you, organised by their native village or area — so when something happens somewhere you don't know well, you can reach out directly to someone with real local context, instead of waiting on the formal chain of command.

**Tactic(s) this serves:** [[Police Wiki/Tactics/Gathering Information\|Gathering Information]]

**Situations this applies to:** Especially valuable when [[Police Wiki/Situations/Taking charge of a New Posting\|Taking charge of a New Posting]] — an unfamiliar district's local knowledge doesn't vanish just because you're new to it, it's already distributed across the people already serving there. Useful any time an incident lands somewhere outside your own direct knowledge.

**Subjects this applies to:** Own personnel (constables and officers); local geography and community knowledge.

## Notes

This came from a senior officer's own practice, early in his career as an SP — he kept exactly this kind of list, constables mapped to native village, so that whenever something happened in an unfamiliar area he could call someone from that place directly and get real context fast, outside the formal reporting chain.

It's a small, personal habit, but it points at something bigger: the organisation already holds an enormous amount of dispersed local knowledge, spread across everyone serving under you. Most of it never gets organised or made retrievable — this is one of the simplest ways to make it retrievable.
