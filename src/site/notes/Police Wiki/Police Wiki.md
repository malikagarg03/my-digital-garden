A practical guide for officers to think clearly and act effectively in real situations.

Built for young IPS and DySP rank officers. Useful for anyone in the field.

## How this is organised

A huge amount of what makes a good officer effective is never written down. It lives inside individual officers' heads — how to read a situation, whom to talk to, how to get someone to open up, how to plan an operation — and it disappears when they move on or retire. This site is an attempt to capture that knowledge properly, and to keep adding to it over time.

Everything here serves one of two purposes: **Investigation** (establishing the truth about something that already happened) or **Law and Order** (managing a threat to public peace that's happening now, or about to). Every tactic and technique below can serve either purpose, or both — which purpose a given technique serves is noted on the technique itself, not baked into the tactic.

Under each purpose sit [[Police Wiki/Understanding Tactics\|Tactics]] — the major things you're trying to achieve. Under each tactic sit [[Techniques.base|Techniques]] — specific, learnable methods for achieving it. Each technique is tagged with the [[Situations.base|Situations]] it's useful in (joining a new posting, a hostile witness, a communal flare-up) and the [[Subjects.base|Subjects]] it applies to (a witness, an informant, a mobile phone, a crowd). Real [[Procedures.base|Procedures]] — actual instances of an officer using a technique in a real case — ground all of it in what actually happened, not just theory. Techniques (or sometimes a whole Situation) can also be linked to the [[Tools.base|Tools]] that support them — a checklist, a piece of software, anything that makes the technique easier to actually execute — with each tool marked as usable offline, online, or both, since knowing what still works without a network signal is real field information.

This structure is loose and still being worked out. It'll get more rigorous as more gets added.
